The Campaign

OVERVIEW

A wedding lead-generation campaign built on one simple insight: the local audience was being spoken to in the wrong language.

Ramadia Ranmal Holiday Resort — a beachside hotel on Sri Lanka's west coast near Moratuwa — was a known wedding venue, but one that lived and died by seasonality. Wedding season runs roughly October to April. May through September is the off-season, when inquiries dry up and bookings vanish.

The brand's social media had drifted. All content was in English, even though the target market was overwhelmingly local Sri Lankans. Engagement was flat. Inquiries from social were almost zero.

The Problem

THE CHALLENGE

Wedding season runs roughly October to April. May through September is the off-season — inquiries dried up, bookings disappeared.

Worse, the brand's social media had drifted:

  • ✕ All content was in English — even though the target market was overwhelmingly local Sri Lankans
  • ✕ Engagement was flat. Inquiries from social were almost zero
  • ✕ The brand had previously run Sinhala content and seen better reach, but abandoned it to "look more professional"

Their actual customers were local Sri Lankans. And local Sri Lankans engage with content in Sinhala — the language they speak, think, and make decisions in.

The Moment

THE TURNING POINT

"Do you want me to run a targeted wedding lead campaign?"

This wasn't a formal brief. It started with a routine check-in.

During a monthly content planning call, I asked the brand manager Mrs. Manjula how business was going. She mentioned the wedding slump — not as a request for help, just as context.

So I asked: "Do you want me to run a targeted wedding lead campaign?"

She said yes.

The best briefs come from listening harder than you were asked to.

04
The Discovery

THE INSIGHT

Two things became clear after pulling up the data:

English content wasn't resonating — engagement was flat, comment-to-inquiry was almost zero.

Sinhala had historically outperformed. They just stopped using it.

The fix wasn't a new design or a bigger budget. It was going back to the language the audience actually speaks.

For a locally focused wedding business, familiarity and emotional connection matter more than appearing "international."

The Plan

THE STRATEGY

01

LANGUAGE

Sinhala copy and captions. Connected on a cultural and emotional level with the local audience.

02

CREATIVE

Clean design with gold Sinhala on a half-blue background. Eye-catching, minimal, built curiosity.

03

PSYCHOLOGY

Teased only a few package details. Curiosity beats information dumps every time.

04

CALL TO ACTION

"Contact us for more details" in Sinhala. Low-friction, direct, easy to act on.

05

TARGETING

Hyper-local: Moratuwa and surrounding areas. Cut CPL by staying local and relevant.

06

PLATFORM

Facebook & Instagram via Meta Ads Manager. Where the audience actually spends their time.

06
How It Happened

EXECUTION

01

Creative Design

Designed a clean, brand-aligned creative from scratch following brand guidelines. Half-blue background, gold Sinhala lettering, minimal on-screen text. No stock-photo glamour. Just the brand in the language that felt like home.

02

Campaign Setup

Built the campaign end-to-end in Meta Ads Manager with tight geo-targeting to keep CPMs low and relevance high.

03

Copywriting

Short, curiosity-driven Sinhala copy ending in a single clear call to action. Every word written to spark interest, not to explain everything.

04

Duration

Ran for 3 weeks — long enough to gather momentum, short enough to maintain urgency.

05

Lead Management

Every single inbound message got a personal reply plus the full wedding package details. No automation. No templates. Just genuine, timely responses.

What I Learned

WHAT I TOOK AWAY

01

Proactive Beats Reactive

The best work shows up before it's asked for. Listening to a client's casual comment and turning it into action created the entire campaign.

02

Language Is Strategy

"Looking professional" was costing them real business. Speaking to people in their own language isn't less professional — it's more effective.

03

Curiosity Is the Best Copy

Withhold more than you reveal. The campaign succeeded because it made people want to know more, not because it told them everything.
